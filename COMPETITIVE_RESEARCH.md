# FactorLab Competitive Research

Research date: 2026-07-05

## Market Map

### 1. Direct invoice factoring fintechs

These products sell funding directly to SMEs and focus on speed, simplicity, accounting integrations, and trust.

- Kriya: invoice upload portal, instant client/buyer authentication, buyer spending limits, up to 90% advance within 24 hours, transparent fees, trust-account settlement, reconciliation, accounting integrations, multi-currency/export support, and merchant portal.
- FundThrough: QuickBooks/OpenInvoice/Xero sync, invoice eligibility dashboard, manual upload fallback, AI/automation for processing, linked-bank payouts, customer-contact workflow, account manager/chat support, and bank-grade data security.
- altLINE: quote funnel, customer login, factoring calculators, submit invoice workflow, 80-90% advance within 24 hours, debtor pays factor, referral partner flows for bankers/brokers/advisors.
- Riviera Finance: online account management, factoring calculator, multilingual entry points, invoice verification, up to 90% advance within 24 hours, collections handled by factor.
- SAP Taulia Receivables Finance: enterprise-grade trust/security, on-demand or regular financing, up to 100% advance, non-recourse for obligor credit risk, no minimum utilization, competitive receivables pricing.

### 2. Factoring operations platforms

These are the deeper benchmarks because they power factoring companies, not just SME acquisition funnels.

- FactorCloud: terms engine, automated schedule creation, payment application, client portal with funding status tracker, schedules with multiple invoices, multiple payout methods, credit reports, credit limits, client/debtor/vendor CRM, UCC/contract/document tracking, NOA templates, buy/no-buy rules, collections pipeline, chargeback management, lockbox import, payment matching, 40+ exportable reports, QuickBooks/API integrations, OCR, credit-data, document, banking, mobile, logistics and telematics integrations.
- Lenvi/RiskFactor positioning: invoice factoring software for risk reduction, operational efficiency, credit-portfolio growth, proactive monitoring, API integrations, lender compliance, KYC services, and regulated lending operations.
- FactorFox positioning: AI factoring software / financial-intelligence platform. Public static page exposed little detail, but positioning suggests AI-assisted underwriting and capital deployment workflows.

### 3. Working-capital / embedded-finance platforms

These are not always pure factoring tools, but they shape buyer expectations.

- Kriya PayLater: buyer authentication, payment terms at checkout, merchant portal, collections, reconciliation, direct API, hosted payment page, ecommerce plugins, PSP integrations, offline payment links and trade-card flows.
- C2FO/Demica/PrimeRevenue/Taulia category: large-network working-capital platforms. Their lesson is network intelligence, enterprise integrations, supplier/buyer portals, and risk/compliance credibility.

## What The Market Treats As Table Stakes

- Invoice intake: manual upload plus accounting sync.
- Eligibility: rules by invoice age, debtor quality, disputes, concentration and payment terms.
- Pricing: advance rate, discount fee, service fee, minimum fee, recourse/non-recourse, reserve and settlement.
- Portal: client dashboard, funding status, invoice status, outstanding reserve, documents, support.
- Debtor workflow: verification, notice of assignment, collections status and communication history.
- Credit operations: buyer/debtor credit limit, client limit, buy/no-buy rules, risk alerts.
- Reconciliation: accounting sync, payments, lockbox/bank matching, reserve release.
- Reports: funding report, credit report, collections report, unapplied receipts, chargebacks, portfolio exposure.
- Trust: transparent fees, clear timeline, security language, human support.

## Feature Backlog For FactorLab

### Phase 1: Best Calculator In The Market

- Multi-invoice scenario builder.
- Editable term sheet with recourse/non-recourse logic.
- Cash-today / reserve / total-cost / effective-APR comparison.
- Bank loan comparison and break-even analysis.
- Eligibility reasons per invoice, not just pass/fail.
- Debtor concentration heatmap.
- Invoice age and payment-term risk model.
- Exportable PDF/CSV term sheet.
- Serbian/English currency and language toggle.

### Phase 2: Client Portal MVP

- Company profile and facility setup.
- Invoice upload with drag-and-drop.
- CSV import plus QuickBooks/Xero-ready schema.
- Funding request workflow: draft, submitted, under review, approved, funded, collected, settled.
- Funding status tracker visible to client.
- Document checklist: invoice, proof of delivery, contract/PO, debtor confirmation, KYC.
- Simple messaging/comments per funding request.
- Transparent fee schedule and reserve-release estimate.

### Phase 3: Underwriting Desk

- Debtor profile pages.
- Client profile pages.
- Credit limits by debtor and client.
- Buy/no-buy rules.
- Concentration limits.
- Dispute, overdue and dilution tracking.
- Approval queue with risk reasons.
- Manual override with audit note.
- Auto-generated notice of assignment.
- Internal notes and decision history.

### Phase 4: Operations And Collections

- Collections pipeline.
- Debtor contact log.
- Payment promise dates.
- Aging buckets.
- Chargeback management.
- Reserve release workflow.
- Payment matching/import.
- Unapplied receipts view.
- Collections templates.
- Portfolio exposure dashboard.

### Phase 5: Differentiators

- AI invoice/document extraction.
- AI risk memo for each funding request.
- Fraud signals: duplicate invoice, altered PDF, suspicious debtor concentration, mismatch between PO/invoice/delivery document.
- Debtor network intelligence: payment behavior across clients.
- Scenario optimizer: "which invoices should I factor to get EUR X at lowest cost?"
- Broker/referral portal.
- API-first embedded factoring widget.
- Industry templates: freight, staffing, construction, wholesale/export.

## Recommended Product Positioning

Do not compete as "another factoring calculator." Compete as:

> A factoring command center that lets an SME know exactly which invoices are fundable, what the cash costs, what documents are missing, and what happens next.

The wedge should be speed and transparency for SMEs, then gradually add operations-grade underwriting and collections modules for factoring providers.
